## The Thesis

You cannot fix a system from within it. You build a parallel one and let people migrate.

The current economic operating system is designed for extraction: extract labor, extract resources, extract attention, extract data. It works brilliantly — for the extractors.

Parallel Society is a different operating system. One where:

- **Your time has value from birth** — not from the moment an employer decides to pay you
- **You know who governs you** — not once every four years, but continuously
- **You support what you know** — not what an algorithm optimized for engagement tells you
- **Your work is yours** — recorded permanently, portable, soulbound to you

This is not utopian. Every component exists today. The work is integration.

## The Five Layers

### 1. Identity — "You are human"
**World ID on Worldchain**

One human, one identity, zero knowledge. You prove you're human without revealing who you are. This prevents bots, prevents sybil attacks, and ensures every human gets exactly one Governance Agent and one birthright TIME allocation.

### 2. Awareness — "This is who governs you"
**Governance Agent on NEAR**

An AI agent that maps your complete governance landscape: elected officials, appointed regulators, applicable laws, upcoming elections, policy changes. It runs continuously, monitoring changes and alerting you before decisions affect your life.

### 3. Economy — "Your time has value"
**TIME Protocol on NEAR + Ethereum**

1 TIME = 1 verified hour of human work. Every human gets 1 birthright TIME per day they've been alive. Workers earn up to 23 more per day. Maximum 24 per day. No exceptions. No whales. No printing press.

### 4. Governance — "Your voice matters"
**Staking on NEAR**

Stake TIME, gain proportional voice. Longer commitment means stronger voice. Governance decisions include: dignity floor adjustments, protocol upgrades, treasury allocation, local allocation standards.

### 5. Coordination — "You're not alone"
**Waku on Logos Network**

Privacy-preserving communication between agents and humans. No central servers. No surveillance. Organized by governance jurisdiction, economic activity, and community identity.
